The immortal jellyfish (Turritopsis dohrnii) is the only known animal capable of reverting completely to its sexually immature, colonial stage after having reached sexual maturity. It achieves this through a cellular process called transdifferentiation.

In 1919, a massive wave of molasses rushed through the streets of Boston at 35 miles per hour (56 km/h) after a storage tank burst. The event, known as the Great Molasses Flood, killed 21 people and injured 150.

A day on the planet Venus is longer than a year on Venus. It takes Venus approximately 243 Earth days to complete a single rotation on its axis, but only about 225 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun.

The Eiffel Tower can grow up to 6 inches (15 cm) taller during the summer. This physical change happens due to thermal expansion, where the iron structure heats up, the particles gain kinetic energy, and the metal takes up more volume.

The Lake Hillier in Western Australia is famous for its bright bubblegum-pink color. Scientists discovered that the unique color is caused by the presence of the organism Dunaliella salina, a type of halophile red algae that thrives in high-salt environments.

The Voynich Manuscript is an illustrated codex hand-written in an unknown writing system. Carbon dating has traced the vellum pages to the early 15th century (1404–1438), but no linguist or cryptographer has ever successfully decoded its contents.
